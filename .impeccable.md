## Design Context

### Users
Personal blog for Ben Smith — a 43-year-old software developer with 15 years of experience. The site serves as a canvas for capturing thoughts: technical deep-dives, essays, and project showcases. Secondary audiences include hiring managers evaluating craft, potential clients, and the developer community. The primary reader is Ben himself — the site should feel like something worth coming back to.

### Brand Personality
**Clean. Precise. Alive.**

Inspired by Jakub Antalik's product design language (Frame.io, Dona.ai, his UI component work) — not his simple portfolio page, but his actual product work. The site should feel like a well-made tool: obvious how to use, rewarding to explore. Voice is confident but not loud. The personality shows through care, not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Typographic blog — single-column, text-first.**

- **Light mode primary:** Off-white (#F7F7F8) background, #1a1a1a text, thin #E5E5E5 dividers
- **Dark mode:** Supported via theme toggle with smooth transitions
- **Font:** Inter throughout, no serifs. Titles at 18px semibold, body at 14-15px, metadata at 12-13px
- **Accent:** System blue (#007AFF light / #4DA3FF dark)
- **Content width:** ~640px for reading, generous 16-24px padding
- **Cards:** Soft 8-12px rounded corners, subtle shadows (rgba 0.05-0.15), thin borders
- **Category system:** Technical (blue), Essay (red #FF6B6B), Project (teal #4ECDC4) — colored text on transparent tint backgrounds

**Anti-references (what NOT to look like):**
Flashy landing pages. Anything that screams "look at me" before content loads. Heavy box-based layouts. Serif fonts. Hero images.

### Design Principles

1. **Clean at first glance, rewarding on engagement.** The site looks simple. Micro-interactions and careful details reveal themselves as you use it. Craftsmanship whispers rather than shouts.

2. **Content is king.** Animations register subconsciously as "that's cool" then get out of the way. Effects never compete with or distract from blog content. When in doubt, reduce opacity or constrain to non-content areas.

3. **Deliberate motion.** Every animation has a purpose — guiding attention, confirming an action, or adding personality. Three layers: micro-interactions (7 categories), reusable animated effects, and ambient/hero moments. Shared animation tokens ensure consistency.

4. **Everything breathes.** Generous spacing, thin dividers instead of boxes, content at comfortable reading width. Never crowded. The whitespace is intentional, not lazy.

5. **Respect the reader.** `prefers-reduced-motion` disables all animations and hides the ambient canvas. Light/dark theme follows system preference with manual override. Reading experience prioritized over visual flourish.
